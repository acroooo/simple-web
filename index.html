César Pelli

Architect

César Pelli (San Miguel de Tucumán, Argentina, 12 de octubre de 1926-New Haven, Connecticut, Estados Unidos, 19 de julio de 2019) was an American nationalized Argentine of great prestige and international recognition. He was the brother of the architect Victor Pelli, who is dedicated to the development of popular housing

He studied architecture at the National University of Tucumán, where he graduated in 1948. Later he began to work in his country, until in 1952 he moved to the United States, along to his wife the architect Diana Balmori, where he obtained American citizenship. There their two children: Denis in 1953 and Rafael in 1956. Between 1964 and 1976 they lived in Los Angeles. Pelli was a partner in the firm of Eero Saarinen and later of Gruen Associates. He was dean of the Yale University School of Architecture between 1977 and 1984. He lived in New Haven, Connecticut and directed the Pelli Clarke Pelli studio. His most famous work is the Petronas Towers, in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, which since 1998 and until mid 2003 they were the tallest buildings in the world with 88 stories and a height of 452 meters. Pelli died on Friday, July 19, 2019 at age 92.

In 1977 Balmori and Pelli had settled in New Haven, where together with Fred Clarke they formed the studio. César Pelli and Associates. The study began with a highly relevant commission: the multi-award winner project for the renewal of MoMA in New York. In the following years the study grows with the World Financial Center and Winter Garden in Battery Park. This consists of four towers and a public square open. The studio won the AIA Gold Medal for Firm of the Year in 1989. Balmori leaves the firm in 1990 to which today his son Rafael is integrated. In 2006 he led the project of the Great Santiago Tower in the Costanera Center complex, the most largest in Latin America and the largest commercial building in Latin America, located in Santiago (Chile). In Madrid, the construction of another design of his was carried out: The Crystal Tower, one of the four skyscrapers that were built in the Cuatro Torres Business Area business complex. It has a height 249 meters above ground, making it the tallest building in Spain, according to data from Council on Tall Buildings and Urban Habitat (CTBUH), and its construction was completed in 2007. In addition, César Pelli took over the construction of the Iberdrola Tower in Bilbao, being the building highest in the Basque Country. He also designed the project for the Seville Tower (Seville), a skyscraper of 178 meters and 43 floors that has become the tallest building in the city of Seville and Andalusia.
